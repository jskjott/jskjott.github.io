Welcome!

In this 1st grade classroom, we'll take a closer look at a knowledge-focused, English Language Arts lesson that features a teacher reading aloud. The Read-Aloud is the core part of the Knowledge Strand, focusing on background knowledge, listening comprehension, analysis of text, and speaking and listening skills. Knowledge domains introduce new and engaging content and follow a consistent structure that allows students to build knowledge within and across domains.

Emperor's empire and the Chinampas

The words emperor, empire, wealth, retreat and palace are introduced in this lesson. CKLA core vocabulary words include a range of academic and domain-related vocabulary (Tiers 1, 2, and 3) that students will hear in context multiple times throughout the domain Read-Alouds. In this lesson, after the introduction of the new vocabulary, the class has a short discussion activity about chinampas (the farms of the Aztec people) which they learnt about in a previous class.

Core Vocabulary

emperor, n. the ruler of an empire
Variation(s): emperors

empire, n. the people and the land controlled by one ruler
Variation(s): empires

palace, n. grand homes for kings queens or emperors
Variation(s): palaces

retreat, v. to move away from something or leave
Variation(s): retreats, retreated, retreating

wealth, n. valuable possessions
Variation(s): none

Check for Understanding

Turn and Talk During the previous lessons, you learned that the Maya had many different rulers, including kings and priests. How is the Aztec civilization different? (Unlike the Maya, the Aztec civilization is an empire, or a group of people and land that is ruled by a single ruler.)

Read-Aloud

1. Introduce complex texts

2. Engage with content knowledge

3. Develop listening comprehension skills


Students are read aloud an engaging narrative or informational text exposing them to new vocabulary, knowledge and language. The lessons and domains are designed to build upon each other, so that students can connect new knowledge and concepts to previous learning. During Read-Alouds, students participate in close reading strategies to develop deeper understanding.


Moctezuma and the Arrival of the Strangers

In today's Read-Aloud we meet Moctezuma, the emperor of the Aztec empire. We hear about how he lives a life in prosperity. Furthermore, we witness how Moctezuma receives the news of the arrival of strangers on the shores of his empire (European colonists). As this story is told images are like the one seen on the right are shown from the Flip Book to provide more context for students to engage with the story and the knowledge it contains.

Comprehension Questions

1. Generate discussion

2. Develop student's ability to ask and answer Literal, Inferential and Evaluative Questions

3. Encourage oral expression

After the Read-Aloud, rich discussion is generated based on content of the text that was just read. The varying nature of the questions-literal, inferential, and evaluative- seeks to develop the students as critically-thinking, active, curious, and discerning participants, expressing their own views while remaining rooted in the rich text.

Word Work

1. Acquire deep knowledge and understanding of select words.

2. Further cement knowledge of content.

3. Develop oral expression.


Each Read-Aloud is accompanied by additional and specific vocabulary activities. During this time the class will explore in a deeper way how a key core vocabulary word from the lesson is used in the world.. In the process students acquire deeper knowledge and understanding while becoming more able to describe the world with rich vocabulary.


Emperor

The Word Work today is focusing on the word emperor. In the first part of the activity the students hear more about what the word means and how it is used. They are then asked to discuss in pairs how their life would be like or what they would do if they were an emperor. The questions on the right are an example of how the lesson plan support these activities by providing additional questions and points of discussion for the class.




Speaking and Listening

Emerging - Have students name their empire if they were an emperor/empress.
Expanding - Have students give a more detailed description of their empire if they were an emperor/empress.
Bridging - Have students give a detailed description of their empire and their life if they were an emperor/empress.

Application

1. Apply what has been learned from the Read-Aloud

2. Develop expressive speaking and writing skills

3. Synthesize new knowledge and skills

Finally students apply the content they learned through a rich variety of speaking, listening, reading, and writing activities. Application activities are based on the content of the Read-Aloud. Students have the opportunity to apply their newly acquired knowledge and vocabulary in the rich context of the domain. . Application activities also provide an opportunity to check for correct use of language in context.
