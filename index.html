Goodmorning!


It is the first lesson of the day which happens to be a ELA lesson based on the CKLA curriculum. This is the 7th lesson in the domain about Early American Civilizations of the First grade knowledge strand of CKLA. Students engage with stories about the Inca empire while connecting this knowledge to a broader history of the Americas. In doing this they develop the knowledge needed to foster good language skills. The lobular which is focussed on during this lesson is related to the knowledge the students engage with in different ways.


Core Vocabulary

emperor, n. the ruler of an empire
Variation(s): emperors

empire, n. the people and the land controlled by one ruler
Variation(s): empires

palace, n. grand homes for kings queens or emperors
Variation(s): palaces

retreat, v. to move away from something or leave
Variation(s): retreats, retreated, retreating

wealth, n. valuable possessions
Variation(s): none

Introducing The Read-Aloud



Support

Image 3A-4: If students have trouble, refer back to the Civilization Chart: Maya and review the Mayan leaders.

Read-Aloud



Show image 7A-1: Moctezuma and his entourage [Point to Moctezuma in the picture] So, Moctezumawas teh emperor of a large empire.

Comprehension Questions

Support

If students have difficulty responding to questions, reread pertinent lines of the read-aloud and/or refer to specific images.

Word Work


Speaking and Listening

Emerging - Have students name their empire if they were an emperor/empress.
Expanding — Have students give a more detailed description of their empire if they were an emperor/empress.
Bridging — Have students give a detailed description of their empire and their life if they were an emperor/empress.
